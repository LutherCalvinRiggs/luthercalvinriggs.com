Hi! I'm Luther.

When you stop and think about it, you can end up spending over 2000 hours each year in the presence of your coworkers. Now imagine that being someone you don't vibe with. Since it can be hard to gauge someone's personality from a resume, I wanted to take a moment and share a little about what makes me as a person.

To put it simply, I've seen a lot in life. I graduated with a degree in finance, only to take a job that would be wiped out three years later by a global market meltdown. At a time of massive, industry-wide layoffs, I humbly returned to one of my collegiate professions: tending bar. From there I found a passion in nightlife, took up DJing as a hobby and ultimately built myself a career in music production for the next decade.Then, as the music was playing its loudest, the plug was pulled on nightlife as the COVID-19 pandemic took the world by storm.

Once again, I returned to my roots, pursuing another creative passion from the past: coding. I can remember the time of dial-up and dot-matrix printers. The original car phones and floppy disks. I wrote my first 'app' in high school so that I could tune my guitar at home. I constructed financial trading algorithms in Excel to test market strategies with historical prices. I even designed my first DJ website using a 'For Dummies" book.

"I'm an artist, and if you give me a tuba, I'll bring you something out of it."

- John Lennon, 1971

I have always been a creative and I have always been a builder. Whether audio or visual, analog or digital, I'm an artist. And I look forward to creating something amazing with you.

Thinking Outside the Box

It is common to think that the most creative people are better at "thinking outside the box". I, however, do not believe that the most creative people think outside of the box. I believe that they have filled their box with more life experiences. Therefore, let me share some of my life experiences with you!

I was born in Witchita, Kansas and raised in Oskaloosa, Iowa. Though I didn't grow up on a farm, my mother's family had over 100 acres and we spent a good amount of our childhood helping with chores. We were a typical midwest family that took road trips in a van and shot fireworks every 4th of July. I learned to play violin and piano, sang in the church choir and played varsity sports. I also participated in regional math competitions and wrote my first app for a guitar tuner on a floppy disk. In my teens, I developed an interest in architecture and drafting, and later began my college education as an architecture major. Later, after encouragement from family, I finished my undergraduate studies with a bachelor's degree in finance and a boarded a one-way flight to pursue a life in the Big Apple.

After a stretch on Wall Street, I was swept up in the waves of layoffs due to the financial market collapse. Knowing the opportunity that New York City still held, I began bartending and looking for my next opportunity. Some friends introduced me to DJing, I caught the bug, and so began a return to my musical roots. Only this time, I didn't want to simply play the songs, I wanted to make my songs. Over the next decade, I played countless DJ gigs and wrote albums of music.

My hobbies include music, travel, photography, athletics, linguistics and cultural explorations. From 'what make people tick' to 'why is coffee so good', I constantly want to understand and live everything that I can. I've experienced a lot in life. Let those experiences work for you.

If you would like to experience more of my life highlights, check out my Instagram.
